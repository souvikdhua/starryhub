--- SECTION 1: PLANETARY STATES & DIGNITIES ---
1.1 EXALTATION (UCHCHA) & DEBILITATION (NEECHA)
- Sun: Exalted 10° Aries. Debilitated 10° Libra.
- Moon: Exalted 3° Taurus. Debilitated 3° Scorpio.
- Mars: Exalted 28° Capricorn. Debilitated 28° Cancer.
- Mercury: Exalted 15° Virgo. Debilitated 15° Pisces.
- Jupiter: Exalted 5° Cancer. Debilitated 5° Capricorn.
- Venus: Exalted 27° Pisces. Debilitated 27° Virgo.
- Saturn: Exalted 20° Libra. Debilitated 20° Aries.
- Rahu: Exalted in Taurus/Gemini. Debilitated in Scorpio/Sagittarius.
- Ketu: Exalted in Scorpio/Sagittarius. Debilitated in Taurus/Gemini.

1.2 NEECHA BHANGA RAJA YOGA (Cancellation of Debilitation)
A debilitated planet's weakness is cancelled and turns into immense success later in life if:
- The lord of its debilitation sign is in a Kendra (1, 4, 7, 10) from Lagna or Moon.
- The planet that would be exalted in that sign is in a Kendra from Lagna or Moon.
- It is aspected by its sign lord.
- It is conjunct the exalted planet of that sign.

1.3 COMBUSTION (ASTANGATA)
Planets lose their ability to protect their living significations when too close to the Sun:
- Moon (12°), Mars (17°), Mercury (14°, or 12° if retrograde), Jupiter (11°), Venus (10°, or 8° if retrograde), Saturn (15°).
- Combust planets cause frustration, hidden talents, and weakness in the houses they rule. However, if the combust planet is Vargottama or in its own sign, the internal intelligence remains intact despite external expression struggles.

--- SECTION 2: HOUSE RULERSHIPS & YOGAS (PARASHARI) ---
2.1 THE HOUSES (BHAVAS)
- TRINES (Trikona - 1, 5, 9): Houses of Dharma, grace, past-life merit, and ease. Always auspicious.
- ANGLES (Kendra - 1, 4, 7, 10): Houses of action, effort, physical reality, and support. 
- DUSTHANAS (6, 8, 12): Houses of suffering, obstacles, transformation, debt, illness, and isolation.
- UPachayas (3, 6, 10, 11): Houses of growth. Malefic planets (Mars, Saturn, Sun, Rahu) perform excellently here, giving the drive to conquer obstacles over time.

2.2 VITAL RAJA YOGAS
- Dharma Karmadhipati Yoga: Conjunction or mutual aspect between the 9th lord (Dharma/Luck) and 10th lord (Karma/Career). The highest yoga for professional supremacy.
- Viparita Raja Yoga: When lords of Dusthana houses (6, 8, 12) are situated exclusively in other Dusthana houses without aspect from benefic lords. Causes sudden rise after a catastrophic failure or through another's ruin.
- Gajakesari Yoga: Jupiter in a Kendra from the Moon. Gives lasting reputation, wisdom, and protection against many afflictions.

--- SECTION 3: JAIMINI SUTRAS (SOUL ASTROLOGY) ---
3.1 CHARA KARAKAS (Working Karakas based on Degree)
1. Atma Karaka (AK): Highest degree planet. The Soul's deepest unfulfilled desire. The King of the chart.
2. Amatya Karaka (AmK): 2nd highest. The Minister. Governs career path, financial sustenance, and the "mind" that executes the soul's desire.
3. Bhratru Karaka (BK): 3rd highest. Siblings, gurus, mentors.
4. Matru Karaka (MK): 4th highest. Mother, home, inner peace.
5. Putra Karaka (PK): 5th highest. Children, creativity, followers, students.
6. Gnati Karaka (GK): 6th highest. Enemies, diseases, obstacles, debt. The "killer" karaka.
7. Dara Karaka (DK): Lowest degree planet. The spouse, partnerships, and business partners.

3.2 KARAKAMSA & NAVAMSA (D9)
- Navamsa (D9) is the chart of destiny, marriage, and the second half of life. It reveals the true, internal strength of a planet.
- Karakamsa: The sign occupied by the Atma Karaka in the D9 chart.
- The 10th house from Karakamsa dictates the supreme career path.
- The 12th house from Karakamsa dictates the path to Moksha (liberation) or spiritual inclination.
- A planet in the 7th from Karakamsa describes the exact psychic nature of the spouse.

--- SECTION 4: VIMSHOTTARI DASHA & TIMING (PHALADEEPIKA) ---
4.1 MAHADASHA (Major Period)
- The Mahadasha Lord sets the stage, environment, and overarching theme for the period (e.g., Venus for 20 years, Sun for 6 years).
- Its natural significations (e.g., Venus = love/luxury, Saturn = delay/discipline) AND its house lordship will dominate the native's life chapter.

4.2 ANTARDASHA (Sub Period)
- The Antardasha Lord delivers the actual specific results and events.
- To read an Antardasha: Count the houses from the Mahadasha lord to the Antardasha lord.
- 6/8 axis (Shadashtaka): If the AD lord is 6th or 8th from the MD lord, the period brings immense friction, enmity, sudden changes, and health issues.
- 2/12 axis: Indicates expenditure, loss, or relocation.
- 5/9 or 1/7 axis: Indicates highly auspicious results, harmony, childbirth, marriage, and fortune.

--- SECTION 5: TRANSITS (GOCHAR) & SADE SATI ---
5.1 GOCHAR PRINCIPLES
- Transits must ALWAYS be read from the Natal Moon (Chandra Lagna).
- Saturn transit over the 1st, 2nd, and 12th house from the Natal Moon is SADE SATI (7.5 years of structural pressure and karmic reckoning).
- Jupiter transit in the 2nd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 11th from the Natal Moon brings extreme luck, marriage, childbirth, and wealth.
- Rahu and Ketu transits across the 1/7 axis from Ascendant or Moon always trigger relationship karmas, sudden obsessions, and detachments.

--- SECTION 6: PRACTICAL REMEDIES (UPAYAS) ---
Astrology is useless without solutions.
- Debilitated Planets: Require fasting on their specific day or donating items of their color. NEVER wear a gemstone for a debilitated planet (it amplifies the toxic energy).
- Combust Planets: Require chanting the Beej Mantra of the planet and propitiating the deity to give the planet "breath."
- Malefics in Dusthanas: Require charity (e.g., Saturn in 8th requires giving food to the unhoused or disabled on Saturdays).
- Weak Benefics: CAN be strengthened with specific, flawless gemstones set in specific metals, worn on the correct finger on the correct day during the waxing moon (Shukla Paksha).

--- SECTION 7: NAVAMSHA (D9) — THE CHART OF DESTINY ---
7.1 NAVAMSHA SIGNIFICANCE
- The D9 chart reveals the TRUE strength of planets. A planet strong in Rasi but weak in D9 = promises that never materialize.
- A planet weak in Rasi but strong in D9 = late-blooming power, strength in the second half of life.
- VARGOTTAMA: A planet in the same sign in both D1 (Rasi) and D9 (Navamsha) is Vargottama — it is exceptionally pure and reliable, like a double-locked promise.
- The D9 Ascendant shows the native's inner self and dharmic path.
- Venus in D9 describes the actual nature of the spouse and marriage quality, not just the 7th house in D1.

7.2 D9 HOUSE ANALYSIS
- 1st house D9 = inner character and dharmic alignment
- 7th house D9 = the actual marriage experience (not just the spouse's personality)
- 10th house D9 = the career that fulfils dharmic purpose
- If D9 lagna lord is strong and well-placed, the second half of life surpasses the first.

--- SECTION 8: DASAMSA (D10) — CAREER CHART ---
8.1 DASAMSA SIGNIFICANCE

- Sun in D10: Career in government, authority, leadership, politics.
- Moon in D10: Career in nurturing, healing, public service, real estate.
- Mars in D10: Career in military, engineering, surgery, sports, police.
- Mercury in D10: Career in communication, writing, trading, technology, media.
- Jupiter in D10: Career in teaching, law, philosophy, religion, consulting.
- Venus in D10: Career in arts, fashion, beauty, entertainment, luxury goods.
- Saturn in D10: Career in manual labor, construction, mining, discipline-based fields, bureaucracy.

--- SECTION 10: ATMAKARAKA — SOUL PURPOSE ---
10.1 ATMAKARAKA PLANETS
- Sun as AK: The soul craves recognition and self-mastery. Must learn humility. Leadership is the path.
- Moon as AK: The soul craves emotional security. Must learn detachment from emotional dependency.
- Mars as AK: The soul craves victory and courage. Must learn non-violence and patience.
- Mercury as AK: The soul craves knowledge. Must learn that words carry consequences.
- Jupiter as AK: The soul craves wisdom and expansion. Must learn that not everyone deserves the same teaching.
- Venus as AK: The soul craves love and beauty. Must learn that attachment is not the same as love.
- Saturn as AK: The soul craves mastery over suffering. Must learn that pain is the teacher, not the punishment.

--- SECTION 11: SADE SATI — THE 7.5 YEAR TRANSIT ---
11.1 THREE PHASES OF SADE SATI
- RISING PHASE (Saturn in 12th from Moon): The pressure begins. Sleep disruption, undefined anxiety, loss of mental peace. The ground is shifting but nothing has visibly changed yet.
- PEAK PHASE (Saturn over natal Moon): The heaviest period. Emotional devastation, financial restructuring, health crisis, loss of a parent or mentor. The entire emotional foundation is demolished and rebuilt.
- SETTING PHASE (Saturn in 2nd from Moon): The worst is over but the scars remain. Financial reconstruction, finding voice after silence, rebuilding family structures. Wisdom emerges from the ruins.

--- SECTION 13: PLANETARY FRIENDSHIPS ---
13.1 PANCHADA (5-FOLD) FRIENDSHIP
- Intimate Friend (Adhi Mitra): Natural friend + Temporal friend. These planets amplify each other's best qualities.
- Friend (Mitra): Natural neutral + Temporal friend. Moderate support.
- Neutral (Sama): Natural friend + Temporal enemy, or Natural enemy + Temporal friend. Mixed results.
- Enemy (Shatru): Natural neutral + Temporal enemy. Friction and obstruction.
- Bitter Enemy (Adhi Shatru): Natural enemy + Temporal enemy. These planets destroy each other's significations.
- When two planets in a chart are Adhi Shatru, the native experiences a deep internal war between the life themes they represent.

--- SECTION 14: ASHTAKAVARGA ---
14.1 SARVA ASHTAKAVARGA (SAV)
- Total benefic points per sign from all 7 planets + Lagna.
- A sign with 25+ SAV points is FAVORABLE for any planet transiting through it.
- A sign with less than 25 SAV points creates DIFFICULTY when planets transit through it.
- Saturn transiting a low-SAV sign during Sade Sati = the most psychologically devastating period possible.
- Jupiter transiting a high-SAV sign = maximum luck, expansion, and fortune.
